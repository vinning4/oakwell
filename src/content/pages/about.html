MBBS, FRACGP

Dr. Noa Dahan

  • Bio

    Dr Noa Dahan graduated from the Technion — Israel Institute of Technology before completing her MBBS in Australia in 2010, becoming FRACGP in 2016, and holding a Postgraduate Certificate in Cosmetic Medicine from the Australasian College of Cosmetic Medicine. She blends general practice with a special focus on cosmetic and skin medicine. She enjoys helping patients feel comfortable in their skin, whether that's through managing pigmentation or providing carefully planned cosmetic treatments. Noa is meticulous about safety and patient education, ensuring every treatment plan is tailored, conservative, and aligned with long-term health.

  • Special Interests

    — Cosmetic medicine, including anti-wrinkle injectables
    — Management of common skin concerns (acne, rosacea, sun damage)
    — Preventive health and routine GP care for adults

  • Languages

    — English
    — Hebrew
    — French
    — Russian

MBBS, FRACGP

Dr. Nguyet Tu

  • Bio

    Dr Nguyet Tu graduated from Hanoi Medical University in Vietnam before achieving FRACGP fellowship in 2011, with additional Certificate training in Women's Health from the Royal Australian and New Zealand College of Obstetricians and Gynaecologists (RANZCOG). She is a compassionate GP with a strong interest in women's health across all life stages, from contraception and fertility through to menopause care. She offers a gentle, thorough consult style and takes time to explore both medical and lifestyle factors that may be affecting wellbeing. Nguyet also provides doctor-led skin rejuvenation treatments, always aiming for subtle, natural results that complement overall health.

  • Special Interests

    — Women's health, fertility and contraceptive counselling
    — Shared antenatal care and postnatal support
    — Skin rejuvenation treatments such as medical peels

  • Languages

    — English
    — Vietnamese
    — Hmong
    — Cantonese

MBBS, FRACGP

Dr. Andrew Blake

  • Bio

    Dr Andrew Blake graduated from Monash University in 1998 with a Bachelor of Medicine/Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) and became a Fellow of the Royal Australian College of General Practitioners (FRACGP) in 2004. He is an experienced family GP who enjoys caring for patients over many years and across generations. He has a particular focus on helping men address health concerns early, from heart risk factors to fatigue and sleep issues, and he works closely with patients to build practical, achievable health plans. Andrew is known for his clear explanations and collaborative approach, making it easier for patients to understand their options and feel confident about their care.

  • Special Interests

    — Men's health and preventative screening
    — Management of diabetes, hypertension and cardiovascular risk
    — Long-term care for chronic conditions

  • Languages

    — English
